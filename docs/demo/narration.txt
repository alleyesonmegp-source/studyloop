Most study apps count minutes. StudyLoop measures what becomes stronger and
explains what to review next.

The learner starts with a subject, exam date, and their own material. Here I
load a complete photosynthesis example. StudyLoop turns those notes into a
daily mission and shows an estimated readiness score based on visible learning
evidence.

The mission begins with a micro-lesson grounded in the notes. A focus timer
supports distraction-free study; then three retrieval questions test what was
actually remembered.

I intentionally answer the first question incorrectly. StudyLoop explains the
correct concept and saves the question in a local rescue queue. Correct answers
strengthen mastery, while the missed concept is scheduled to return. The final
result shows recalled concepts and updated exam readiness.

The Loop view makes the next action explainable: unresolved mistakes receive
priority until recovered. Progress shows mastery, recall evidence, focus
history, experience points, and readiness—not only time spent.

Coach AI is optional. GPT-5.6 Sol transforms a bounded topic and notes into a
schema-validated micro-lesson and exactly three age-appropriate questions. The
API key remains on the FastAPI backend, never in the APK. Responses use store
false, and a labeled offline fallback keeps the app demonstrable.

Codex was used throughout OpenAI Build Week to design the adaptive engine,
implement Flutter and FastAPI, fix small-screen overflows, create tests,
translate the competition build, and prepare the public repository.

StudyLoop uses AI to create useful practice, while transparent evidence decides
what returns next. Focus. Recall. Adapt. Repeat.
